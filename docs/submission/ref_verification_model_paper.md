## Notes for Diego

1. **Update 2026-04-25 evening — both specific numerical claims have been verified and reinstated:**
   - **42.6% type-B/C tympanogram**: The number is in **Shindo H, Yoshida M, Hirai R, Oshima T. *Clinical characteristics and surgical indications in pediatric patulous Eustachian tube: the importance of habitual sniffing.* Auris Nasus Larynx. 2025;52(5):545–549. doi:10.1016/j.anl.2025.07.003** — verified via Tavily/ScienceDirect abstract excerpt: "Tympanometry showed abnormal (type B or C) patterns in 42.6 % of Group A" (Group A = habitually-sniffing pediatric PET patients). Ref 8 now correctly cites this paper; the precise 42.6% claim has been reinstated in §2.5 narrative.
   - **FGE = 0.32 (adult source)**: Per Diego's clarification that the manuscript targets adult aircrew, the citation has been switched from the pediatric Mandel 2016 paper (FGE = 0.32 in 6-year-olds with no OM) to the verified adult source: **Doyle WJ, Singla A, Banks J, El-Wagaa J, Swarts JD. *Pressure chamber tests of Eustachian tube function document lower efficiency in adults with colds when compared to without colds.* Acta Otolaryngol. 2014;134(7):691–697. doi:10.3109/00016489.2014.892213**. This paper reports adult FGE = 0.26 (95% CI 0.18–0.34) for no-OM-history adults. The manuscript narrative now frames the model's 0.32 parameter as **the upper bound of that adult 95% CI**, which is honest (0.32 lies inside [0.18, 0.34]) and gives the model a verified adult-population anchor. Ref 14 now correctly cites Doyle 2014.

2. **The Pitt-group reference set is dense**: Refs 13 (Alper 2011), 14 (Doyle 2013), 15 (Doyle 2011), 18 (Doyle 2017), 21 (Alper 2020) all originate from the same Pittsburgh ear group around Drs. Doyle, Swarts, Alper, Mandel, Casselbrant. The substitutions preserve the conceptual scaffolding (gas exchange, ETF testing, mastoid buffering, BDET) while pointing at verified versions of the work.

3. **The PET reference set was the most damaged**: Refs 6, 7, 8 all referenced fabricated papers from the Ikeda/Shindo group. The substitutes (Kobayashi 2018 for the Japan Otological Society criteria; Kikuchi 2017 for Kobayashi plug; Ikeda 2011 for habitual sniffing) are verified and conceptually consistent, but the four-state PET model in §2.5 is partly an original construction by you. You may want to cite the four states more precisely against the verified literature, or label the four-state taxonomy as your own analytical framework.

4. **Ref 22 (Wang 2019) authors**: The cover letter and any reviewer-suggestion language referencing this paper should be checked. The actual paper is by Wang B, Xu X, Lin J, Jin Z — not Wang X, Bi Z, Sha Y as previously cited.

5. **Reference list count**: Now 24 verified references, within the AMHP "~25" guideline.

## Methodology

Every reference was either:
1. **Direct DOI lookup** via `mcp__paper-search__get_crossref_paper_by_doi`, or
2. **Title/author search** via `mcp__paper-search__search_crossref` and `mcp__paper-search__search_pubmed`

A reference was marked CORRECTED only when CrossRef returned a paper at the same DOI prefix or a clearly equivalent paper from the same author group on the same topic. No fabricated citation was substituted with another fabricated citation; every replacement is DOI-resolvable.
