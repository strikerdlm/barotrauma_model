### Principal novelty and relevance to AMHP

To our knowledge this is the first published incidence analysis of hypobaric-chamber ear barotrauma in an aerospace-medicine program conducted from a high-altitude resting baseline. The Colombian Aerospace Force DIMAE chamber operates at 2,640 m (8,530 ft), the highest institutional baseline altitude of any major national aerospace-medicine training program. The published chamber-barotrauma literature — including the three Italian Air Force cohorts (Morgagni 2010, Morgagni 2012, Landolfi 2009) and recent Finnish commercial-aircrew survey work (Lindfors 2021) — is drawn entirely from sea-level or near-sea-level installations. Whether a high-altitude-resident cohort experiences comparable, higher, or lower chamber-barotrauma incidence is of direct operational interest to every Latin American, Andean, and Himalayan aerospace-medicine program, and of comparative interest to the international community.

The principal finding is a pooled 2010–2026 per-exposure incidence of **2.38%** (Wilson 95% CI 2.06–2.75%; 6.97% projected career-3) — statistically indistinguishable from the Italian Air Force envelope (1.5–2.4%) and stable across the 15-year window between the 2010–2020 case registry and the 2025–2026 operational capture. The manuscript also reports two secondary findings of operational interest: (i) preflight screening-flag discriminatory analysis, locating 93% of denial decisions in the high-discrimination respiratory, ear/URI, fever-malaise, and chronic-disease flags; and (ii) a cross-sectional blood-pressure and SpO₂ distribution from 1,004 preflight evaluations at 2,640 m baseline, a reference distribution that has not been published for a Latin American military aircrew population.

### Relation to a companion manuscript

A physics-informed middle-ear-barotrauma model calibrated against this FAC cohort is under concurrent review at this journal ("Physics-Informed Middle Ear Barotrauma Risk for Hypobaric Chamber Training," submitted 2026). The cohort paper and the model paper are designed to stand alone: the cohort paper publishes the empirical FAC incidence on its own epidemiological merits; the model paper cites the cohort paper as its calibration anchor. Either can be reviewed and published without dependency on the other. I note the companion manuscript for editorial awareness and any author-network handling.

### Originality and prior publication

This manuscript has not been submitted or published elsewhere, in whole or in part. The 2010–2020 case registry was originally compiled by co-authors Correa and Jaimes in a 2022 internal DIMAE document that was never submitted to a peer-reviewed journal and is not considered prior publication. The present manuscript substantially extends that registry (adding the 2025–2026 Phase 2 operational dataset, de-identified case-level analysis, preflight screening and vital-signs characterization, and the pooled 16-year statistical analysis) and is offered as the first peer-reviewed publication of this cohort. The 2025–2026 dataset is analyzed here for the first time. No part of the work has been posted to any preprint server.

### Statistical expertise

All statistical computations — Wilson 95% confidence intervals on binomial proportions, case-denial discrimination ranking, vital-signs descriptive statistics under plausibility filtering, and career-exposure projections under stated independence assumptions — were implemented and executed by the author in Python 3.11 with NumPy 1.26 and SciPy 1.11. Where small-sample or zero-event Wilson intervals degenerate, they are reported as such with the n-count shown, without imputing a point estimate. The de-identified tidy CSVs are available under the companion repository so any reader can reproduce the analysis.

### Ethics, regulatory classification, and data

Under Colombian Resolution 8430 of 1993 of the Ministry of Health (*"Por la cual se establecen las normas científicas, técnicas y administrativas para la investigación en salud"*), Article 11(a), this retrospective secondary analysis of de-identified DIMAE operational records is classified as *investigación sin riesgo* (research without risk) and does not require institutional-ethics-committee submission or individual informed consent. The study was conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki (2013 revision). **Publication of this work was authorized by the Colombian Aerospace Force.** Raw case-level data with Colombian national identifiers (Cédula), names, and institutional emails remain under the data-protection boundary of the Colombian Aerospace Force; further data may be shared with qualified researchers upon written authorization from the Colombian Aerospace Force institutional authority. De-identified aggregate CSVs and analysis scripts are publicly available in the companion repository (<https://github.com/strikerdlm/barotrauma_model>, under `docs/Cohort FAC/analysis/`).
