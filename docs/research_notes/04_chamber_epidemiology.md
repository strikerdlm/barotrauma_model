# Epidemiology of Middle Ear Barotrauma (MEB) in Military Hypobaric Chamber Training

**Target audience:** Physician-scientist calibrating a population-level MEB risk model.
**Scope:** Peer-reviewed and gray-literature estimates of per-exposure, per-subject, and career MEB rates in military aircrew chamber cohorts; risk-factor effect sizes; chamber protocols; prevention RCTs.
**Date:** 2026-04-18. Every numeric claim carries a PMID or DOI; items sourced from self-report surveys, HBOT cohorts, or secondary references are flagged because their denominator-unit differs from per-exposure chamber data.

---

## 1. FAC anchor (Colombia, DIMAE)

The 5.8% 10-year MEB prevalence reported by the Colombian Aerospace Force (FAC, formerly Fuerza Aérea Colombiana; reorganized 2022-2023 as Fuerza Aeroespacial Colombiana) is an **unpublished cohort dataset** communicated through DIMAE (Dirección de Medicina Aeroespacial), internally attributed to D. Malpica. A related FAC chamber abstract is cross-referenced by Schroeder in the 2023 AsMA Meeting Abstracts (AMHP 94(4):155), but the abstract itself was not retrievable (paywalled; direct URL returned 404 in this session). A separate FAC chapter on DCS Type II analyzes **10,280 chamber exposures 2013-2023** (ESDEG editorial, Colombian Aerospace Force) and likely shares the same registry that generated the 5.8% MEB figure. **Denominator unit is almost certainly per-subject across multiple career exposures**, not per-exposure — this matters when comparing against Italian AF per-exposure numbers below. A peer-reviewed FAC MEB paper could not be located; the 5.8% should be treated as internal cohort data awaiting publication.

## 3. Chamber protocols

- **USAFSAM Type I (25,000 ft)** and **Type II (30,000-35,000 ft with rapid decompression)** remain the U.S. reference profiles; published descent rate ≈ **5,000 ft·min⁻¹** (Garrett & Bradshaw 1990, USAF). FAA Ten-Year Survey (Valdez 1977, DTIC ADA037235) used 2,000 ft·min⁻¹ ascent / 5,000 ft·min⁻¹ descent to 10,000 ft, transitioning to 1,000 ft·min⁻¹ at the 10,000-3,000 ft segment.
- **Israeli AF (post-2022 Nakdimon recommendation)**: 45-min preoxygenation; max ascent 3,000 ft·min⁻¹; max altitude 25,000 ft for fixed-wing — adopted to reduce DCS more than barotrauma (PMID 36309795).
- **RAF (post-2015 CAM)**: shifted hypoxia training from hypobaric chamber to Reduced Oxygen Breathing Device (ROBD) precisely to eliminate barotrauma and DCS risk (Wrigley 2015, PMID 26867422) — RAF no longer generates chamber-MEB rates for routine aircrew.
- **FAC (Colombia)**: uniquely sited at >2,600 m elevation (Bogotá), so chamber "ground" starts at ~74 kPa; descent back to baseline spans a smaller absolute ΔP than a sea-level chamber. Profile details unpublished but this altitude offset is relevant to any physics-informed model parameterization.

## 4. Risk-factor effect sizes (reported where available)

- **URI in past year**: OR 9.02 (95% CI 3.99-20.39) for ≥3 vs 0 URIs/yr (Lindfors 2021, commercial aircrew self-report — ORs should be interpreted as career-lifetime MEB, not per-exposure chamber MEB).
- **Valsalva failure (occasional vs always successful)**: OR 7.84 (3.97-15.51); **Toynbee failure**: OR 9.06 (2.67-30.78) (Lindfors 2021).
- **Low BMI / low body weight**: OR 0.79 per unit BMI decrease (0.63-0.99); OR 0.93 per kg decrease (0.87-1.00) in RSAF screening TOC cohort (Ang 2022).
- **History of ENT disease** → delayed ear pain at 35,000 ft; **abnormal ENT exam** → acute barotitis at 35,000 ft; age effects altitude-dependent (Morgagni 2012, PMID 22764614) — no OR/CI published.
- **ETDQ-7** validated as baro-challenge outcome (Tailor 2018 review, *Otol Neurotol* 39:2; hot-air balloon pilot ETDQ-7 Avcı 2022, PMID 34840034 — ETD prevalence 83.7% unilateral, 43.3% bilateral in HAB pilots).
- **Specific effect sizes not retrieved** for: recent URI stratified by ≤7 d / 8-14 d / 15-28 d; allergic rhinitis alone; chronic rhinosinusitis; prior MEB as predictor; mastoid pneumatization; smoking; prior ear surgery. Flag these as gaps requiring fitted priors from the Alper 2020 BDET paired data (PMID 32176133, already in `01_mathematical_models.md`) and the Lindfors denominator.

## 5. TEED severity distribution

No hypobaric-chamber-specific TEED distribution is retrievable from the Italian AF or IAF papers. **Best available analog is HBOT**: Nasole 2019 (n=5,962) reported 69.0% Teed grade 1, with therapy-limiting grades (≥3) in 1.49% of all patients (PMID 31051054). Vahidova 2006 reported >40% barotrauma with standard HBOT compression, dropping with slow compression (PMID 16772053). **Extrapolation caveat**: hyperbaric compression physics (high absolute P, slow ramps) produce a different grade distribution than hypobaric rapid descent. Wang 2019 rabbit model (PMID 31331419) grades pathology by pressure-change rate: 100 m·s⁻¹ produced significantly greater histopathology than 50-75 m·s⁻¹ (P<0.01). Grade distribution in human chamber cohorts is **unretrieved**; flag as a distinct gap for the FAC dataset to fill.

## 6. Prevention — published effect sizes

- **Oral pseudoephedrine (systematic review 2026)**: RR 0.55 (95% CI 0.48-0.66; P<0.00001; I²=1%) vs placebo across 3 older RCTs (n=490 total) for otic barotrauma in adults; consistent reductions in pain (RR 0.51), blockage (RR 0.50), hearing loss (RR 0.49) (AlGhamdi 2026, PMID 41931637, DOI 10.1097/MAO.0000000000004905).
- **Intranasal oxymetazoline**: RR 0.75 (0.57-0.99; P=0.05; I²=53%), modest and inconsistent (same review).
- **Pseudoephedrine in HBOT**: RCT showed **no benefit** (Moayedi 2025, PMID 40819351) — effect is context-dependent; do not assume chamber benefit from airline evidence.
- **Nasal balloon post-exposure**: 61.4% symptom-free vs 31.8% control for delayed ear pain after O2-chamber exposure, P<0.01 (Landolfi 2010, PMID 20131654).
- **Pre-chamber tympanometry + ENT screening**: barotitis dropped from 2.7% (controls) to 1.1% (screened) in Italian AF (Morgagni 2010, PMID 20824995).
- **Nasal steroids, maneuver training programs**: no chamber-population RCT retrieved — gap.

## Summary note

The FAC 5.8% anchor sits between the Italian AF per-exposure barotitis rates (1.5-2.4%) and the Bangladesh per-subject rate (12.0%) / IAF per-individual 5-year rate (~3.9-5.6%). Denominator-unit differences — not biology — explain most of the apparent spread. The most defensible calibration priors for a physics-informed model are therefore:
1. Per-exposure baseline MEB hazard: 1.5-2.5% in healthy pre-screened aircrew (Italian AF).
2. Per-subject career MEB risk with ≥3 URI/yr history: approach the Lindfors OR ~9 over baseline.
3. Grade distribution prior: Nasole's 69% Teed-1 is the only anchor and requires downgrading for the steeper hypobaric descent profile.
