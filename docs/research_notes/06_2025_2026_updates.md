# 2025-2026 Evidence Update for Physics-Informed MEB Risk Model

**Scope**: Feb 2025 onward; evidence (a) updating quantitative parameters, (b) introducing new physics, (c) providing external validation, or (d) contradicting current priors.
**Baseline audited**: v2.2.0 (Kanick-Doyle 2005; Doyle 2017; Alper 2020; Mandel 2016; Wang 2019; Morgagni; Landolfi; Buchman; Ikeda 2020/24; Shindo 2025; Ghadiali; Malik & Ghadiali; Lindfors 2021).
**Date**: 2026-04-18. Databases: PubMed, Europe PMC, Semantic Scholar (scite rate-limit-blocked).

---

## Actionable findings

### 1. Pseudoephedrine does NOT prevent MEB in HBOT — Moayedi 2025 (PMID 40819351)
RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled. No difference in ear pain, TM injury, or rescue use.
- **Confidence**: High.
- **Delta**: Contradicts the implicit model prior that sympathomimetics reduce MEB, extrapolated from diver/airline literature.
- **Action**: Set prophylactic pseudoephedrine effect to null (OR 1.0, narrow CI); tag SCUBA/airline extrapolations as indication-specific.

### 2. BDET meta-analyses — Swords 2025 Cochrane (PMID 40008607) and Khan 2026 (PMID 41776716)
Swords: 9 RCTs, 684 pts, ≤3 mo ETDQ-7 MD **-1.66 (-2.16 to -1.16)**; tympanometry RR **2.51 (1.82-3.48)**; sham-controlled MD **-0.54 (-2.55 to 1.47, NS)**; 3-12 mo uncertain. Khan: 23 studies, pooled n=309, 1-year ETDQ-7 MD **-2.03 (-2.59 to -1.47)**; no LA vs GA difference.
- **Confidence**: High (Cochrane), Medium (Khan).
- **Delta**: First pooled short-term estimates; sham-controlled signal null — durable benefit conditional on non-sham comparator.
- **Action**: Update BDET prior to RR 2.51 (objective restoration) and MD -1.66 short-term / -2.0 at 12 mo vs non-surgical; flag regression toward baseline vs sham.

### 3. ET bidirectional pumping + tympanic-orifice vortex — Zhang 2025 (PMID 41092566)
PIV of CT-derived PET tube models under varied MEP: at onset of negative intraluminal pressure, **simultaneous suction from both ends toward the center**; vortex at the tympanic orifice during opening under ME negative pressure.
- **Confidence**: Medium (in-vitro; PET-derived anatomy).
- **Delta**: Current Kanick-Doyle/Doyle 2017 assumes unidirectional opening-driven clearance. Bidirectional pumping + vortex implies a modulation of per-swallow FGE when P_ME < P_nasopharynx (descent).
- **Action**: v2.3.0 roadmap — sensitivity axis on per-swallow clearance efficiency during descent; not a first-order change.

### 4. HBOT otologic adverse-event MA — Voigt 2025 (PMID 41429031)
54 studies, n=18,284. 15% otologic events; MEB 42.8% mild, 6.4% severe. Risk factors: age, female sex, H&N pathology, **sensory neuropathy**, pre-treatment equalization difficulty.
- **Confidence**: High.
- **Delta**: Sensory neuropathy is new; age/female/H&N-pathology confirm priors. Model's Nasole 2019 9.2% MEB anchor remains internally consistent (Voigt's 15% covers all otologic).
- **Action**: Add sensory neuropathy as a categorical risk modifier; recalibrate overall HBOT-MEB estimate with Voigt pooled uncertainty.

### 5. HBOT MEB predictors — Lee 2025 (PMID 40364015; PMID 40288902)
Two cohorts, n≈300 each, monoplace HBOT. Altered mental status OR **3.16 (1.05-9.52)** and **2.50 (1.13-5.51)** independently across both; emergency-status OR **6.75 (1.33-34.2)**; albumin >4.3 and higher Mg protective.
- **Confidence**: Medium (retrospective, but convergent across two cohorts).
- **Delta**: Impaired volitional equalization (altered mental status) is a novel, mechanistically coherent risk modifier.
- **Action**: Add "impaired volitional equalization" covariate (applies to sedated HBOT, intoxicated or unconscious aeromedical evacuation).

### 6. 3T MRI+CBCT ET anatomy — Holm 2026 (PMID 41466069)
n=28 ETD vs 10 controls. Longer ET (p=0.035); **smaller Ostmann's fat pad (OFP) and levator veli palatini (LVPM) muscle volume** in ETD ears — consistent with atrophy.
- **Confidence**: Medium (small n, but first quantitative multimodal).
- **Delta**: Quantifies a structural susceptibility prior previously qualitative.
- **Action**: If anatomic covariate added to v2.3.0, parameterize from OFP thickness and LVPM volume rather than age alone.

### 7. Large-scale PET risk factors — Oshima 2025 (PMID 41014990)
n=1009 patients, 1613 ears, Japan Otological Society criteria. Habitual sniffing OR **8.18**; weight-loss history OR 1.78; female + low-dose estrogen-progestin OR **7.31**; TM flutter OR **42.17**. Seven-symptom model AUC 0.862. **Distinct paper** from Shindo 2025 PET entries already in the baseline (PMID 40779854 pediatric PET; Oshima 2025 is a different larger adult cohort from the same Japanese group).
- **Confidence**: High (largest PET cohort to date).
- **Action**: Update PET risk-weight parameters to Oshima ORs; add estrogen-progestin exposure as a female-specific covariate.

### 8. GLP-1 agonist-induced PET — Sudhoff 2025 (PMID 40721956)
Case series, n=7. Semaglutide/tirzepatide weight loss 8.2-18.7% → PET.
- **Confidence**: Low (case series).
- **Delta**: Novel pharmacological PET trigger, mechanistically coherent with OFP atrophy.
- **Action**: Add GLP-1 exposure binary covariate; await cohort confirmation before high weighting.

### 9. Free-fall paratroopers — Dubey 2025 (PMID 41821584)
Pre/post, n=30, 1,000 ft in 6 s (≈3,000 m·min⁻¹). **No significant MEB** (Stuart-Maxwell χ²=2.00, p=0.368).
- **Confidence**: Low (small n; possible pre-selection).
- **Delta**: At rates an order of magnitude above Nakdimon's 3,000 ft/min caution, null outcome is consistent with quasi-adiabatic brief-transient physics rather than mucosal-gas-exchange-limited damage — supports Wang 2019 rate-dependence framework.
- **Action**: Weak prior-strengthening; confirms model assumption that sub-10-s transients behave as quasi-adiabatic.
